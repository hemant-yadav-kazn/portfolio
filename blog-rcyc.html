HY. / BACK TO BASE
Hospitality Log: RCYC

Be Hungry.
Be Humble.

"Growth comes from leaning into discomfort, not dodging it."

This summer, waiting tables at the Royal Canadian Yacht Club taught me more than I expected. It wasn’t just a job; it was a crash course in showing up, RAW and REAL.

I walked in nervous on my first shift, my hands shaky with a tray of flute glasses. By July, I was chatting with sailors and members like old friends, their stories of racing in rough waters pulled me out of my shell. It's the members who welcomed me into their club.

Precision in the details

Every guest carried something—a bad day, a big win, a quiet loneliness, and an expectation of EXCELLENCE. I learned to spot it in their eyes and offer a smile or a quick refill to lighten the load.

Mistakes hurt. A misheard order or a spilled drink felt like the end of the world to me. But every slammed shift, every fumble, was a chance to carve out STRENGTH. I learned to own it—"My bad, let me fix that for you"—and found people appreciated the HONESTY.

I figured out how to stay calm, to move with PURPOSE and intention. My coworkers were my LIFELINE. We had each other’s backs, clearing plates or running drinks when someone had a busy section. That’s when I got it: a team isn’t just people working together, it’s people who see you and step up.

The environment demands excellence

I started to see hospitality as more than food and drink; it’s about giving a damn about each and every small detail. And yes, it matters!

The Best Moment?

A retiree lingering over coffee, sharing a story of a lost friend with me. He didn’t need a rushed service; he needed space. I poured decaf and listened, not because it was my job, but because it MATTERED.

The menu became second nature. Pair the prime rib with a bold Malbec, not just because it works, but because it feels right. Starting with "Hey, I am Hemant, here to make your night great" wasn’t a line; it was a promise that earned me TRUST and GENUINE connections.

Leadership

None of this would’ve been possible without my managers, Saleem Malik, CCM and Stephen Tung. Big thanks for the opportunity to grow under your strong leadership. Your steady guidance and high standards pushed me to find my CONFIDENCE.

It showed me I could adapt, connect, and lead NATURALLY. I’m taking that into whatever’s next—whether it’s running events or pitching ideas, I know how to read a room and DELIVER.

The future’s wide open, and I’m ready to give it everything, with a little humility to keep me GROUNDED.
